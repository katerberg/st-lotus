St Lotus

Rulings

Over the course of many Vintage Rotisserie Drafts, we have found quite a few edge cases regarding the rules that have needed to be clarified.

Current rulings and divergences are listed below.

1:

Each player gets 46 picks

Cards are picked in order from first seed through eighth seed, and then from eighth seed back to first seed.

Each player may select 46 cards over the course of the draft and may not select any card that has been already taken.

If a player selects a card that has been taken by another player, they may select a different card. If it is noticed several picks later, they may not choose a card that has been taken in the interim and are limited to the remaining cards only.

2021-10-02: Changed to 46 picks instead of 45 to prevent first and last pick going to first seed.

Updated 2021-10-02
2:

Decks are 40 cards

Players must build a deck containing a minimum of 40 cards. They may include any number of “Basic” cards in addition to the cards that they pick.

Any cards not in the “Main Deck” of 40 or more cards is considered the “Sideboard” and allows cards such as Death Wish to retrieve them during a game. The “Main Deck” must be selected prior to the first match and must be the same for the first game of all matches in the tournament.

2023-02-28: Added all “Basic” lands to the list of free cards.

2021-10-02: Added “Wastes” to the list of free cards.

Updated 2023-02-28

4:

“Self-tutoring” cards are picked like any other

Cards like Nesting Wurm or Avarax require a pick to be made in order to play them, and only give the picker a single copy of them. It is not possible to have more than one copy of a card in a card pool.

2023-02-28: Removed exception due to low-power level and rule complexity.

2019-09-08: Updated allow more than one copy of some cards in the interest of increasing their playability.

Updated 2023-02-28
5:

“Draft” cards do not function

Cards that reference “draft” such as Cogwork Spy do not have an effect because during a rotisserie draft, cards are “picked” rather than “drafted”.

Similarly, cards that reference “packs” such as Canal Dredger do not have a useful function since there are no packs that are picked from.

Deal Broker still functions as it would during a booster draft.

Updated 2021-08-01
6:

“Any number” cards are picked like any other

Cards like Relentless Rats require a pick to be made in order to play them, and only give the picker a single copy of them. It is not possible to have more than one copy of a card in a card pool.

2023-02-28: Removed exception due to low-power level and rule complexity.

2021-11-06: Changed to 25 copies at most to allow for mill to still be a potential option.

2021-10-02: Updated allow more than one copy of some cards.

Updated 2023-02-28
7:

“Stickers” and “Attraction” cards function like constructed

Stickers and Attractions follow the constructed rules and do not need to be picked. Only vintage-legal stickers and attractions are available.

Players can pick any 10+ unique cards for their Attraction deck, and any 10+ unique Sticker sheets. At the start of each game, they randomly select three of their Sticker sheets to use for that game.

If a player needs an Attraction or Sticker and did not choose any for their deck (likely due to a Clone effect), they get their own copy of the same Attraction deck or set of Sticker sheets that their opponent selected, and selects three Sticker sheets at random.

2023-07-09: Makes language less verbose.

2023-02-28: Adds clarification of the “Clone” situation.

2022-10-07: Follows constructed rules.

Updated 2023-02-28
